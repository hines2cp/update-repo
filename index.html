About

Claire is an Interactive Design student within the competitive School of Media Arts and Design, preparing for a career in marketing and media management within the music industry. During her time at James Madison University, she has had the opportunity to study and work abroad, take music industry classes and gain knowledge through her design experience. Here's just a few things that she has been working on during her time as a design student.

On this page, you can browse through some designs for social media that Claire has worked on for her internship at an independent record label, called "Sunday Best Recordings" in London, England. Her tasks ranged from social media planning, to writing press releases, to taking inventory of vinyls and merch. There are also several of Claire's latest projects displayed on this page, check it out!

Learning in London

Living in London

In the Fall semester of 2019, Claire packed her bags to begin her junior year in London, England. It was here that she learned how to navigate and live in a city on her own, as well as begin a career as a designer in the music industry.

Social Media Designs

As aforementioned, Claire was tasked with creating unique posts for the label's Instagram and Facebook page. This is just one of the many examples of her creative work at the label.

Press Release

While working in London, Claire wrote her first press release for a brand new song by the band "Xylaroo," a sister duo represented by Sunday Best Recordings.

Crossing the Pond

Claire's travel experiences abroad inspired her to create a book that featured composite images of London and Harrisonburg. This was an assignment for SMAD 332, a class focused on graphic design.

Design in 2020

Latest Projects

2020 has been a year of struggle and growth, which has become evident in Claire's design skills. She has advanced as a student and a designer through collaboration with classmates, input from professors and self-taught methods (thanks to YouTube).

Here's some of Claire's most recent projects.

Rock Co Entertainment

For her concert promotion and production class, Claire designed a logo for a student-made entertainment company, Rock Co Entertainment.Being the only SMAD student in her class, she was able to execute the logo design using her skills from the Interactive Design program.

Sage Sunglasses

For her "Interactive Design for the Web" class, Claire was tasked with coding a retail website for an imaginary brand. She then decided to create a fake sunglasses brand called "Sage Sunglasses." The page had a consistent aesthetic and included fun graphics (like the one above) to portray a professional website.
